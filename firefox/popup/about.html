If you send a letter in the mail and someone other than the intended recipient opens it, that letter is no longer private. This is bad!

Sadly, the same thing can (and does) happen on the internet.

  • Man in the middle attacks
  • Message logging
  • Insecure messaging services
  • Unauthorized account access
  • Vulnerable computer networks
  • And more...

Simply enter your message on Secure Send and set a password. We will lock your message using AES encryption (an algorithm used by the US Government and worldwide). This transforms your message into garbled text that no one can read unless they unlock the message with your password.

Next, send the Secure Send generated link to the message recipient. They will be prompted to enter the password to view the message and (if they enter the correct password), they will be able to read the message.

It’s that simple!

Your message is encrypted on your computer, so you don’t have to worry about us (or anyone else) seeing your message. We don’t keep any record of your message or save it on our servers.

⚠ NOTE:
You should share your message password with the intended recipient out-of-band, meaning not through the same messaging platform you use to share the message link.

For example, if you email the link to your message, tell the recipient the password somehow other than email (in-person, telephone, SMS messaging, etc.)
